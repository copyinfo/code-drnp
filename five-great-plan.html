FIVE Great Plan
Things to 1st Cut
Proposal Success!

1.   Story Time…YOURS…in a Summary

Tell your story… what are all the Do More Good projects you could do with help money from your funders? Tell them why they should sponsor you. This is your Cinderella story! MAKE IT POWERFUL!  Give that funding program officer a reason to dig deeper and deeper into his money chest to grow your projects. Two to four hundred words are all that are standing between you and Doing More Good FOR YOUR EACH AND EVERY PROJECT! Be clear, specific and concise! Your story needs to be told.

2.   How are you going to Do More Good?

Program officers need to know…What they are getting for putting their money with you and your projects.
  • Make your words clear like glass. Make your words help them see exactly what they get when they spend their money with your projects.
  • Make every word count – each word is worth money to you – be concrete and state measurable results for each project!

3.   Connect the money dots…with your Story’s goals.

  • Tell your money Story for connecting your goals. Your budget story needs to help the program officer see the path over the bridge from their money to your Do More Good goals all being completed.
  • Connect your project strategies with how the money is being used for your organization’s efforts to complete your Do More Good  vision.

4.   Budget Smoothie…yummy,tasty numbers

Spread your Spreadsheet with every, every, EVERY expense you write into your Story. Show the program officer why your numbers dig deeper into DOING MORE GOOD than anyone else’s proposal.
  • Smoothly account for everything you are spending their money for.  Do not leave anything out!
  • The Program Officer has a team of financial wizards who wave their very accurate, magical number wands over each budget. Check your numbers twice, three times, and then again.
  • “Magical number wands” have an automatic, “If it doesn’t fit, we dump it!” button. Be careful and complete.
  • Make sure your numbers are accurate everywhere and well blended with everything you propose for Doing More Good.

5.   Be early, early, and REALLY early!

Message sent to your funding Program office and soon-to-be partner: 
  • You plan well + you beat deadlines = you get things done! “The early bird gets the worm,” is true with funding request proposals.
  • Most proposals come screeching in the door at the 11th hour.  Not yours…you want to DO MORE GOOD for your organization.
